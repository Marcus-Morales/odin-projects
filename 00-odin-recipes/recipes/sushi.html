Sushi Recipe

Description

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw fish, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. It is often served with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi. Common types of sushi include nigiri (slices of fish on rice), maki (rolled sushi), and sashimi (slices of raw fish).

Ingredients

  • Seaweed
  • Rice
  • Raw fish

Steps

  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a pot, combine the rice and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 20 minutes.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Cook over medium heat until the sugar and salt dissolve. Allow to cool.
  4. Transfer the cooked rice to a large bowl. Gradually fold in the vinegar mixture with a wooden spoon. Let the rice cool to room temperature.
  5. Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap. Place a sheet of nori on the mat, shiny side down.
  6. With wet hands, spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.
  7. Place your desired fillings in a line across the middle of the rice.
  8. Using the bamboo mat, carefully roll the sushi, applying gentle pressure to keep it tight.
  9. Once rolled, use a sharp knife to slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces.
  10. Serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi.
